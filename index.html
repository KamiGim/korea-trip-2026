🧥 Weather Tips

February in Seoul: Cold and dry, often below freezing at night. Pack a warm coat, gloves, and scarf.

Indoor heating: Korean buildings are well-heated (ondol floor heating). You may want layers to adjust between cold outdoors and warm indoors.

Wind: The cold wind between buildings can be biting. A windproof layer helps a lot.

Street food paradise! Try everything on sticks — cheese, squid, tornado potatoes. Also great for kyochon chicken.

Hongdae (홍대)

Youth food scene — trendy restaurants, late-night eats, unique cafes. Try the 24-hour food alleys.

Upscale dining, premium Korean BBQ, and sleek modern cafes. Garosu-gil for brunch spots.

COEX / Starfield (코엑스)

Massive underground mall with food courts, bakeries, and restaurants from all over Korea.

💡 Travel Tips for Seoul

🚇 Transportation

T-Money Card: Buy at the airport or convenience store. Works on subway, buses, and even taxis. Saves 10–20% vs cash.

Subway: Clean, fast, cheap. Download Naver Map or KakaoMap for navigation. Avoid rush hours (8–9 AM, 6–7 PM).

Buses: Color-coded (blue = trunk, green = feeder, red = express). Pay with T-Money.

💰 Money

Cards: Widely accepted everywhere, even small shops. Visa/Mastercard work best.

ATMs: Global ATMs at 7-Eleven, CU, GS25 convenience stores. Withdraw KRW directly.

Tipping: Not expected! Prices include service. Leaving a tip can sometimes cause confusion.

📱 Tech

SIM/eSIM: Get a Korean SIM at the airport or order an eSIM beforehand (Airalo, Ubigi). T-money is on some eSIMs.

WiFi: Free WiFi everywhere — cafes, subway stations, even on some buses.

Must-have apps: Naver Map (Google Maps is limited), Papago (translation), KakaoTalk (messaging).

🏨 Accommodation

Check-in: Usually 3 PM. Check-out: 11 AM. Ask nicely and some places are flexible.

Hotels: Hongdae and Myeongdong are great central areas. Insadong for a quieter vibe.

Tip: Look for jjimjilbang (찜질방) bathhouses — cheap overnight stays if you're adventurous!

🍽️ Dining

Calling servers: Press the call button on your table or say "여기요!" (Yeogiyo!) — don't wave or snap fingers.

Water: Free filtered water is usually self-serve. No need to buy bottled water at restaurants.

Banchan: Side dishes are free and refillable. Korean BBQ places give you tons!

Tipping: Not expected at restaurants either.

🛍️ Shopping

Tax-free: Spend ₩15,000+ and show your passport at Global Blue counters. Get refund at airport.

Bargaining: Normal at traditional markets (Namdaemun, Dongdaemun). Not at malls or brand stores.

Best areas: Myeongdong (cosmetics), Dongdaemun (fashion night markets), Hongdae (unique finds).
