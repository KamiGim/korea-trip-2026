Myeongdong (명동)

Seoul's iconic shopping district — cosmetics, street food, and vibrant night lights.

Cheonggyecheon Stream (청계천)

A peaceful urban stream running through downtown. Perfect for an evening stroll.

🏯 Day 2 — Sunday, Mar 1

일요일 — Palaces & Universities
Gyeongbokgung Palace (경복궁)

The grandest of Seoul's Five Grand Palaces. Don't miss the Royal Guard Changing Ceremony!

Tip: Rent a hanbok nearby for free entry + gorgeous photos.

Bukchon Hanok Village (북촌한옥마을)

Traditional Korean village with centuries-old hanok houses. Walk the narrow alleys between Gyeongbokgung and Changdeokgung.

Edae (이대)

Trendy university area near Ewha — great for affordable shopping, cafes, and people-watching.

Ewha Womans University (이화여자대학교)

Stunning Gothic-style campus with the famous "Valley" building embedded into the hillside.

Hongdae (홍대)

Seoul's youth & indie culture hub. Street performances, buskers, endless cafes, and nightlife.

🌃 Day 3 — Monday, Mar 2

월요일 — Namsan & Seoul Tower
Namsan Park (남산공원)

Beautiful park on Namsan Mountain. Walk the scenic trails through the forest right in the city center.

N Seoul Tower (엔서울타워)

Iconic Seoul landmark. Take the cable car or hike up for panoramic city views. Love locks at the base!

Best: Visit at sunset for golden hour over the city.

🍢 Must-Try Street Food

Tteokbokki (떡볶이)

Spicy rice cakes in gochujang sauce — Korea's #1 street food. Chewy, spicy, addictive.

Best at: Myeongdong street stalls

🌶️ Spicy 💰 Budget

Odeng (오뎅)

Fish cake skewers in warm broth. Perfect winter comfort food — and the broth is free!

Best at: Any street stall

🐟 Seafood 💰 Budget

Hotteok (호떡)

Sweet pancake filled with brown sugar, cinnamon & nuts. Crispy outside, gooey inside.

Best at: Namdaemun Market, Myeongdong

🍬 Sweet 💰 Budget

Gyeranppang (계란빵)

Egg bread — a fluffy, slightly sweet loaf with a whole egg baked on top. Warm & filling.

🍳 Breakfast 💰 Budget

Gimbap (김밥)

Korean seaweed rice rolls — like sushi's cousin. Grab from any convenience store for a quick bite.

🍙 Snack 💰 Budget

Bungeoppang (붕어빵)

Fish-shaped pastry filled with sweet red bean paste. A beloved winter street snack.

🍬 Sweet 💰 Budget

🍲 Korean Restaurant Classics

Korean BBQ (고기구이)

Grill your own samgyeopsal (pork belly) or galbi (beef ribs) right at the table. Wrap in lettuce with ssamjang!

Best areas: Hongdae, Gangnam, Myeongdong

🥩 Meat 💰💰 Mid-range

Samgyetang (삼계탕)

Ginseng chicken soup — a whole young chicken stuffed with rice, ginseng & jujube. Hearty & healthy.

Famous: Tosokchon (near Gyeongbokgung)

🍗 Soup 💰💰 Mid-range

Bibimbap (비빔밥)

Mixed rice bowl with vegetables, egg, meat & gochujang. Stir it all together for perfection.

Try: Jeonju-style bibimbap

🍚 Rice 💰 Budget

Kimchi Jjigae (김치찌개)

Kimchi stew with pork & tofu — the ultimate Korean comfort food. Every restaurant has its own recipe.

🌶️ Spicy 💰 Budget

Fried Chicken & Beer (치맥)

Korean fried chicken is legendary — double-fried for maximum crunch. Pair with cold beer at a chimaek joint.

Best at: Yeouido Hangang Park (delivery!)

🍗 Fried 🍺 With Beer

Naengmyeon (냉면)

Cold buckwheat noodles in icy broth — refreshing and surprisingly perfect even in winter.

🍜 Noodles ❄️ Cold

☕ Cafes & Desserts

Korean Cafe Culture (카페)

Seoul's cafe scene is next-level. Theme cafes, rooftop cafes, hidden alley cafes — each one an experience.

Areas: Ikseon-dong, Yeonnam-dong, Seongsu-dong

☕ Coffee 📸 Instagrammable

Bingsu (빙수)

Shaved ice dessert piled with toppings — mango, green tea, or classic red bean. Share one (they're huge!).

Try: Sulbing, Cafe Bora

🍧 Dessert 🍓 Sweet

Dalgona Coffee (달고나 커피)

Whipped coffee over iced milk — born in Seoul and went viral worldwide. Try the real thing!

☕ Coffee 📸 Trendy

📍 Neighborhood Food Guide

Myeongdong (명동)

Street food paradise! Try everything on sticks — cheese, squid, tornado potatoes. Also great for kyochon chicken.

Hongdae (홍대)

Youth food scene — trendy restaurants, late-night eats, unique cafes. Try the 24-hour food alleys.

Gangnam (강남)

Upscale dining, premium Korean BBQ, and sleek modern cafes. Garosu-gil for brunch spots.

COEX / Starfield (코엑스)

Massive underground mall with food courts, bakeries, and restaurants from all over Korea.
